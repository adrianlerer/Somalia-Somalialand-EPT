## Natural Experiment Validity

**Somalilandia vs. Somalia Federal** provides an ideal natural experiment because:

1. **Common Historical Baseline (1960-1991)**:
   - Both were part of Somali Republic 1960-1991
   - Shared colonial legacies (British Somalilandia, Italian Somalia)
   - Identical cultural/linguistic/religious context
   - Both experienced Siad Barre dictatorship (1969-1991)

2. **Divergence Point**: **1991 State Collapse**
   - Somalilandia declared independence (May 18, 1991)
   - Somalia remained internationally recognized but stateless

3. **Institutional Divergence**:
   - **Somalilandia**: Bottom-up reconstruction (clan conferences), hybrid xeer-state system, new constitution 2001
   - **Somalia Federal**: Top-down international interventions, transitional governments (2000, 2004, 2012), provisional constitution still not finalized (2025)

4. **Control for Confounds**:
   - Geography: Similar terrain, both have coastline
   - Demography: Both Somali clans (though different clan dominance)
   - Economy: Both pastoralist/livestock-based + ports
   - External threats: Both faced Al-Shabaab, though Somalia more severely

## Constitutional Analysis Summary (Task 1)

### Somalilandia Constitution 2001

**Amendment Procedures** (Article 126):
- Proposal: 1/3 of either House or President
- Threshold: **2/3 of BOTH Houses** (separate votes)
- Waiting period: 2 months after necessity vote
- Failed proposal: Cannot reintroduce for 12 months

**Entrenched Provisions** (Article 127 - Eternity Clauses):
1. Islamic Sharia principles
2. Territorial integrity
3. Democratic/multi-party system
4. Fundamental rights

**Judicial Review**:
- Supreme Court has constitutional supremacy (Article 128)
- Independent judiciary (Article 37, 97-98)
- No separate Constitutional Court

**Xeer Integration**:
- Customary law operates informally (~60-70% of disputes)
- House of Elders includes traditional authorities
- Parallel to state courts (not formally codified)

**Track Record (2001-2025)**:
- **No major constitutional amendments** passed
- Suggests **de facto high entrenchment** despite moderate formal threshold

### Somalia Provisional Constitution 2012

**Amendment Procedures** (Articles 132-137):
- **Dual system**: Different procedures before/after first Parliament term
- **Exact thresholds unknown** (Articles 132-137 text not accessible)
- Final constitution requires **national referendum** (not yet held)

**Federal Structure**:
- Explicitly federal republic (Article 1, Article 3.3)
- Federal member states have constitutional status (Articles 48-54)
- **Unclear if federalism is entrenched** (no explicit eternity clause found)

**4.5 Clan Formula**:
- **NOT found in constitutional text**
- Likely in transitional schedules (not accessible)
- Operates as political practice for seat allocation

**Judicial Review**:
- Constitutional Court created (Articles 109B-109C)
- Explicit invalidation power (Article 4.2)
- **Operational status unclear** - court may not be functional due to instability

**Track Record (2012-2025)**:
- **ZERO major constitutional amendments** passed in 13 years
- Constitution still "provisional" (never finalized via referendum)
- Suggests **extreme de facto rigidity**
