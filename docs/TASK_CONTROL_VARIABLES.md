## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**Data Availability**: Somalia Federal statistics are available from World Bank, IMF, UN, and Somalia National Bureau of Statistics (SNBS). **Somalilandia data is SYSTEMATICALLY BUNDLED** with Somalia in all major international databases, creating a significant data gap for natural experiment analysis.

**Recognition Penalty**: Somalilandia's lack of international recognition results in:
1. **No separate World Bank/IMF country code** → bundled with Somalia
2. **No separate UN statistics** → bundled with Somalia  
3. **Limited official statistics** → Somalilandia Ministry of Finance publishes some data but not systematically
4. **Berbera port revenue** → Not publicly reported separately

**Data Quality**: Somalia Federal = HIGH (IMF, World Bank, SNBS verified sources); Somalilandia = LOW to MEDIUM (Wikipedia estimates, occasional government reports).

## RECOGNITION PENALTY ASSESSMENT (200 WORDS)

**How does bundling of Somalilandia data in international statistics affect empirical analysis?**

The systematic bundling of Somalilandia statistics with Somalia Federal in international databases (World Bank, IMF, UN Population Division, UNCTAD) creates a **severe measurement error problem** for natural experiment analysis. This "recognition penalty" has three critical implications:

**1. Impossible Control Variable Disaggregation**: Key control variables (GDP, remittances, FDI, international aid) cannot be separately measured for Somalilandia, violating the fundamental requirement of natural experiments to isolate treatment effects. This forces reliance on Wikipedia estimates or occasional Somalilandia Ministry of Finance reports, which lack the verification standards of World Bank/IMF data.

**2. Endogeneity Bias**: The bundling creates reverse causation concerns. If Somalilandia's better governance attracts disproportionate aid/FDI (measured within "Somalia" totals), we cannot distinguish whether (a) lower CLI → better governance → more aid, or (b) more aid → better governance independent of CLI.

**3. Attenuation of Treatment Effects**: Bundling dilutes the CLI-governance correlation. For example, if Somalilandia receives 30% of Somalia's total remittances ($520M) but this is coded as "Somalia," the CLI-remittance relationship is attenuated by measurement error.

**Methodological Implication**: The natural experiment's internal validity relies on the exogenous shock (1991 state collapse) creating divergent constitutional paths. However, the recognition penalty means we cannot fully control for economic confounds, weakening causal claims. This limitation must be disclosed in Section 6 (Robustness) with explicit acknowledgment that **Somalilandia's lack of recognition creates both the treatment (unrecognized status → bottom-up constitutionalism) AND the data gap (unrecognized status → bundled statistics)**. This is a feature, not a bug, of the natural experiment but limits our ability to rule out alternative explanations.

## RECOMMENDED APPROACH FOR SECTION 6 (ROBUSTNESS)

Given the **recognition penalty** and systematic data bundling, Section 6 should:

1. **Transparent Disclosure**: Acknowledge that Somalilandia data is bundled with Somalia in all major international databases, creating measurement error.

2. **Somalia Federal Controls Only**: Use Somalia Federal control variables (GDP, aid, remittances, FDI) as **upper bound** estimates, acknowledging that these include Somalilandia but are dominated by Somalia Federal (population ratio ~3:1).

3. **Sensitivity Analysis**: Test CLI-governance correlations:
   - **Model 1**: No controls (bivariate CLI vs Freedom House, CLI vs conflict deaths)
   - **Model 2**: Year fixed effects only
   - **Model 3**: Somalia Federal controls (GDP per capita, aid, remittances) - interpret as "bundled" controls
   - **Model 4**: Somalilandia estimates (Wikipedia GDP per capita $775-$912) - interpret as "exploratory"

4. **Qualitative Triangulation**: Use expert reports (World Bank Somalia Country Economic Memorandum, UNDP Somalilandia governance reports, ICG state-building analysis) to qualitatively assess whether economic differences could explain governance gap.

5. **Natural Experiment Logic**: Emphasize that the 1991 exogenous shock (regime collapse) created similar economic starting points (both poor, conflict-affected) but divergent constitutional paths. Economic differences post-1991 are potentially endogenous to CLI (better governance → more aid/investment).
