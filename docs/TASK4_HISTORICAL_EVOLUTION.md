   - **Somalilandia**: Bottom-up clan conferences ‚Üí permanent constitution (2001)

### Phase 2: CRITICAL DIVERGENCE (1991-2001) - Formative Decade

#### 2.1 SOMALILANDIA PATH (1991-2001): Bottom-Up Constitutionalism

| Date | Event | Institutional Innovation | CLI Impact |

| **May 18, 1991** | Burao Grand Conference | SNM declares independence, revokes 1960 Act of Union | Creates blank slate |

### 2.2 Juncture 2: 1977-1978 Ogaden War (Shared)

**Event**: July 1977 - March 1978 - Somalia invades Ethiopia's Ogaden region  
**Outcome**: **Devastating military defeat**  
- Soviet Union switches support to Ethiopia (sends 12,000 Cuban troops)
- Somalia loses war, economy collapses, $1 billion in Soviet aid withdrawn
- Siad Barre's legitimacy fatally undermined

**Institutional Impact**:
1. **1979 Constitution**: Barre seeks to broaden political base through new constitution (presidential system formalized)
2. **Clan favoritism escalates**: Barre increasingly relies on Marehan/Darod clans, marginalizes Isaaq (north)
3. **Militarization**: Army used for internal repression, clan-based insurgencies begin (1980s)

**Effect on Both Jurisdictions**: ‚úÖ IDENTICAL immediate impact, but **seeds northern grievances** (future Somalilandia)

**Significance**: War bankruptcy creates **preconditions** for 1991 collapse, but does NOT predetermine institutional divergence.

### 2.3 Juncture 3: 1988 Hargeisa Bombing (Divergence Begins)

**Event**: May-June 1988 - Government aerial bombing of Hargeisa and Burao  
**Trigger**: Somali National Movement (SNM) attacks from Ethiopia  
**Outcome**: 
- **50,000-200,000 Isaaq civilians killed** (estimates vary)
- Hargeisa 90% destroyed
- Massive refugee crisis (400,000+ flee to Ethiopia)

**Institutional Impact**:
- **Northern regions**: Siad Barre's brutality creates **unanimous anti-government sentiment** ‚Üí SNM gains total northern support
- **Southern regions**: War continues but no comparable atrocity ‚Üí multiple warlord factions compete

**Divergence Mechanism**:
- **North (future Somalilandia)**: Shared trauma + single liberation movement (SNM) = **cohesion** ‚Üí clan unity possible
- **South (future Somalia)**: Multiple warlords + factional fragmentation = **no cohesion** ‚Üí elite pacts necessary

**Significance**: First **asymmetric event** that creates different institutional possibilities post-1991.

**Reality Filter**: Tier 3 (death toll estimates vary, but event well-documented) - **HIGH confidence on event, MODERATE on exact numbers** ‚ö†Ô∏è

**Sources**: Africa Watch 1990 report, academic consensus

### 2.5 Juncture 5: 2000 Arta Conference (Somalia Federal Path Lock-In)

**Event**: May-August 2000 - Somalia National Peace Conference (Djibouti)  
**Participants**: 810 delegates (clan elders, diaspora, civil society)  
**Outcome**: Transitional National Government (TNG) established

**CRITICAL INSTITUTIONAL INNOVATION: 4.5 Formula**
- **Hawiye clan**: 61 parliamentary seats
- **Darod clan**: 61 seats
- **Dir clan**: 61 seats
- **Rahanweyne clan**: 61 seats
- **All minority clans combined**: 61 seats (hence "0.5")
- **Total**: 305 seats (245 temporary TNG members)

**Institutional Consequences**:
1. **Mathematical Rigidity**: Formula **cannot be modified** without unanimous consent of 4 major clans
2. **Minority Marginalization**: 0.5 status creates **permanent second-class** citizenship
3. **Electoral Impossibility**: Direct elections would violate formula ‚Üí indirect elections **mandatory**
4. **Ultraactivity Mechanism**: Formula persists **25 years** (2000-2025) despite multiple constitution rewrites

**CLI Impact**:
- **CE escalates**: Any constitutional change requires 4.5 compliance ‚Üí **HIGH (0.80)**
- **UA maximizes**: Formula **never modified** despite being "transitional" ‚Üí **VERY HIGH (0.85)**

**Why No Equivalent in Somalilandia?**
- Somalilandia used **proportional clan representation** informally (Guurti), but NOT mathematically rigid
- Presidential/parliamentary elections are **direct popular vote** ‚Üí no formula lock-in

**Reality Filter**: Tier 2 (International Crisis Group, UN documents) - **HIGH confidence** ‚úÖ

## 5. MECHANISMS OF PATH DEPENDENCE

### 5.1 Somalilandia: ADAPTIVE PATH DEPENDENCE

**Mechanism**: **Positive Feedback Loop** (virtuous cycle)
1. **Clan conferences (1991-1993)** create **broad legitimacy**
2. **Legitimacy** enables **voluntary disarmament** (SNM dissolves)
3. **Reduced violence** allows **economic recovery** (livestock trade, Berbera port)
4. **Economic stake** motivates **political participation** (2001 referendum 99.9% turnout)
5. **Participation** reinforces **institutional legitimacy** (elections accepted despite delays)
6. **Legitimacy** enables **judicial review** (2023 Supreme Court can challenge president)

**Result**: **Self-reinforcing democracy** - each institutional success makes next success more likely

**CLI Implication**: **Lower CLI (0.54)** maintained because institutions **respond to feedback** (term extensions controversial ‚Üí elections held)

---

### 5.2 Somalia Federal: OSSIFYING PATH DEPENDENCE

**Mechanism**: **Negative Feedback Loop** (vicious cycle)
