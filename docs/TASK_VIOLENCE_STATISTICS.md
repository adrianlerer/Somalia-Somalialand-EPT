## DATA SOURCES & ACCESS

### UCDP (Uppsala Conflict Data Program)
- **URL**: https://ucdp.uu.se/downloads/
- **Coverage**: 1989-2023 (annual updates)
- **Indicator**: Battle-related deaths (state-based conflict only)
- **World Bank Indicator**: VC.BTL.DETH (https://data.worldbank.org/indicator/VC.BTL.DETH)
- **2023 Somalia Data**: 3,847 battle-related deaths
- **Limitations**: Does not include one-sided violence, non-state conflict, or criminal violence

### ACLED (Armed Conflict Location & Event Data)
- **URL**: https://acleddata.com/
- **Coverage**: 1997-present (weekly updates)
- **Indicator**: Political violence events (battles, explosions, violence against civilians, protests, riots)
- **Somalia Report**: "Context Assessment: Heightened Political Violence in Somalia" (March 3, 2023)
- **Key Data**: August-December 2022 = 1,200+ events, 3,850 fatalities
- **Advantages**: Granular event-level data, includes non-state violence
- **Limitations**: "Reported fatalities" may differ from UCDP's "battle-related deaths" due to methodology

### UNOCHA (UN Office for Coordination of Humanitarian Affairs)
- **URL**: https://www.unocha.org/somalia
- **Coverage**: 2015-present (monthly situation reports)
- **Indicator**: Humanitarian needs, displacement, civilian impact
- **2024 Report**: 5.98 million people need humanitarian assistance in 2025 (down from 6.9 million in 2024)
- **Limitations**: Focuses on humanitarian impact rather than conflict deaths; no annual death count aggregates

### News Outlets (Major Incidents)
- **Reuters**, **CNN**, **Al Jazeera**, **BBC**, **NPR**: Used for verifying specific major incidents (Mogadishu 2017, Las Anod 2023)
- **Reliability**: High for confirmed deaths from official sources (probe committees, government statements)

## DATA QUALITY ASSESSMENT (200 WORDS)

**Overall Quality**: **MODERATE**

The violence statistics for Somalia suffer from three critical limitations that affect the robustness of the Somalia-Somalilandia natural experiment:

**1. Somalilandia Data Bundling**: UCDP and World Bank datasets code Somalia as a single entity, bundling Somalilandia fatalities with Somalia Federal fatalities. This creates measurement error for the natural experiment, as Somalilandia's lower violence (~40 annual deaths) is masked by Somalia's high violence (~3,000+ annual deaths). Manual disaggregation using ACLED's event-level data is required but labor-intensive.

**2. Methodological Discrepancies**: UCDP counts "battle-related deaths" (state-based conflict only), while ACLED counts "political violence fatalities" (including non-state conflict, one-sided violence, explosions). This creates inconsistent annual totals. For example, ACLED reports 3,850 fatalities for August-December 2022 alone, while UCDP's 2023 total is 3,847. Different inclusion criteria explain the gap.

**3. Las Anod Attribution Ambiguity**: The Las Anod conflict (2023-ongoing) occurs in Somalilandia-claimed territory but involves non-state actors (SSC-Khatumo militia). UCDP may code this as "Somalia" conflict, while it directly affects Somalilandia's violence statistics. Attribution decisions significantly impact CLI-violence correlations.

**Recommendation**: Use **ACLED event-level data** with manual filtering for Somalilandia events + **UCDP annual aggregates** for Somalia Federal baseline. Document bundling limitation in paper's "Data & Methods" section.
